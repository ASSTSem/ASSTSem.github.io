Gus Chang

Gus is the CEO, IC and System Application. Previsouly, he was the CTO of SLSI (A 2.6M+ Startup). In 2017, Gus and the SLSI Team won 3 first place awards in microelectronics in Sunnyvale (4-15-17), in Boston (5-15-17), and in ChangChun, China (6-23-17) consecutively. Due to miss communications, trade war eroded IC and IT business, the CEO of SLSI could not win any VC in China. He is a serial inventor and was responsible for the creation of the IBM Trench Isolation 1976 IEDM and the SCMOS Device 2001.

Some of Gus' Achievements:

  • CTO of SLSI (2.6M+ Startup)
  • Invented a novel new material making a low threshold Schottky barrier diode LtSBD™
  • Invented the SCMOS Device 2001
  • Stanford University Graduate

Gus had his second super innovative ideas formed in 2001 on making the Schottky CMOS or Super CMOS (SCMOS™) chips. He invented a novel new material making a low threshold Schottky barrier diode LtSBD™, integrated it with transistors, making this as a basic SCMOS™ circuit structures to implement all advanced IC system functions. The SCMOS™ circuits may replace any conventional CMOS circuits, supersede them, and make all IC chips running faster (2X), reduce the chip area (2X), and save the power/heat (4X). According to our simulations, compounded system benefits are (2X2X4=16 fold, or 1600% better than CMOS), and cheaper. This leads to another quantum jump and disruptive IC inventions, which shall boost IOE market explosively successful in the next decades to come.
